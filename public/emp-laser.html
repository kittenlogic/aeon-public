FAQ

What is the differences between JPT and Raycus?

The major differences between JPT and Raycus has to do with the pulse width and the frequency available for each.

Typically speaking a JPT source will allow more control over the beam and allow for whiter whites on aluminum, darker blacks on steels, and brighter colors on plastics. If the goal is to have brighter colors on polymers or have darker colors on steels than a JPT is well worth the upgrade.

If you simply want to dig into metal with an engrave, or just do some simple names on tools, either is a great option.

What's the difference between the 30W or 50W models?

The difference in power is important to anyone trying to do deep engraving, coins, cutting very thin metals, etc. It is also important as lenses get wider.

Light follows the inverse square law, so as area doubles the intensity of light is a function of the square root of that. Not important that you know the math, but the photo in the Wiki article should clear it up.

Essentially you need much more power if you want to go wider, so if you want to engrave a 300mm wide object, it will require way more power than if you are trying to do a 150mm object.

30W is fantastic for most engraving that isn't deep, but anytime you want to go deep or cut thin metals (think aluminum foil, pennies, etc) you will usually want a smaller lens and a 50W or higher.

How does laser output power affect productivity and ROI?

Because a galvo laser can move so quickly, the ability to have more power is usually a nice thing to have as we can go faster for the given job and still deliver the same engrave. Galvos go over the same area over and over again to produce the job or cut desired, so having the increase in power shortens the timeframe required to complete the task.

Which model is best for annealing steel?

Annealing steels produce a black color on the surface and is much easier to do on a JPT source unit.

Can you get colors with EMP fiber lasers?

We can not achieve colors on a standard Fiber laser, that will require a MOPA laser on steel, something which is very difficult to reproduce consistently.
