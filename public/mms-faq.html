File Setup

What unit of measurement should my files be set to?

What unit of measurement should my files be set to?

Your files should be set to inches (in) or millimeter (mm).

Note: This is critical; our laser cutters cannot process files set to other units of measurement.

What is the minimum size requirement for bridging in the designs?

What is the minimum size requirement for bridging in the designs?

The minimum width of the rectangle used for bridging is 1mm (3/64”).

What is the maximum file size for upload?

What is the maximum file size for upload?

The maximum file size you can upload is 10mb per file.

Can I nest multiple designs in one file?

Can I nest multiple designs in one file?

We recommend that you only nest non-identical parts in one file (e.g. a custom ornament with different names on each; or an assortment of different parts). Important: Be sure to leave 3mm (1/8") between parts.

If you want to order multiple quantities of the same part, please only upload the design for that part and select how many parts you wish to order. Note: we offer volume discounts when ordering multiples of the same part.

How to prevent dense nodes or anchor points in my file?

How to prevent dense nodes or anchor points in my file?

Our software will identify these points and assist with simplifying them. If you need help, you can always email your file to support@mymetalshop.com and we can identify them manually or assist as well.

Can you help with my design?

Can you help with my design?

Please see our design guidelines for step-by-step guidance to prep your files. If you need additional help with best practices and tips, email support@mymetalshop.com. Note: At this time we do not offer full design services.

Can you send the scraps or dropouts from my parts?

We can if you request them, but due to the laser passing over the parts, it's likely they will be damaged or have some unwanted cuts within them. You will be quoted a new shipping price for the drops.

Will my laser cut sheet metal parts be perfectly flat?

Will my laser cut sheet metal parts be perfectly flat?

Will my parts be tapered?

Will my parts be tapered?

Depending on the thickness of the cut, there will be little to no perceptible taper.

Do I need to compensate for kerf in my design?

Do I need to compensate for kerf in my design?

No, the machine will automatically compensate for kerf. If you compensate for kerf this will make your part larger than requested.

Will my parts have burr and dross?

Will my parts have burr and dross?

Lasers have the advantage of cutting parts extremely precisely with minimal dross. All parts will go through a deburred process if you request it, and although we try to make every edge smooth, it is possible that some parts will have sharp edges.
